# S5 — India Telecom: Sector Source Map

**Sector:** India Telecom
**Created:** 2026-02-26
**Source ID Range:** S51 -- S100

## 1. Regulatory / Government

### S51 — TRAI: Telecom Subscriptions Reports
- **URL:** https://www.trai.gov.in/release-publication/reports/telecom-subscriptions-reports
- **Frequency:** Monthly
- **Key Data:** Wireless/wireline subscriber counts by operator, market share, net additions, urban vs rural split, broadband subscriber data, teledensity
- **Reliability:** High
- **Access:** Free / no registration
- **Notes:** Gold-standard source for Indian telecom subscriber data. Most recent release covers data as of 31 Dec 2025 (released 10 Feb 2026). Reports typically lag by 6-8 weeks.

### S52 — TRAI: Performance Indicators Reports
- **URL:** https://www.trai.gov.in/release-publication/reports/performance-indicators-reports
- **Frequency:** Quarterly
- **Key Data:** Revenue (AGR, GR), ARPU, MOU, data usage per subscriber, capex, EBITDA margins, interconnect traffic, broadband speeds, QoS metrics
- **Reliability:** High
- **Access:** Free / no registration
- **Notes:** The most comprehensive regulatory dataset on Indian telecom financial and operational performance. Typically released with a 3-4 month lag (e.g., Q1 2025 data released Dec 2025).

### S53 — TRAI: Consultation Papers & Recommendations
- **URL:** https://www.trai.gov.in/release-publication/consultation
- **Frequency:** Ad-hoc (10-15 per year)
- **Key Data:** Spectrum pricing recommendations, tariff frameworks, interconnection regulation, OTT regulation, net neutrality, broadcasting tariffs
- **Reliability:** High
- **Access:** Free / no registration
- **Notes:** Critical for tracking policy direction. Recent key paper: spectrum auction recommendations for 2026 (released Feb 2025) covering 600 MHz, 6 GHz bands. Consultation on PM-WANI broadband tariffs (Jan 2025).

### S54 — TRAI: Tariff Orders
- **URL:** https://www.trai.gov.in/telecom/telecom-tariff
- **Frequency:** Ad-hoc
- **Key Data:** Floor/ceiling tariffs, IUC rates, broadband tariff frameworks, tariff amendment orders
- **Reliability:** High
- **Access:** Free / no registration
- **Notes:** Tariff orders directly impact operator ARPU and profitability. Most recent: 71st Amendment Order (2025) on PM-WANI broadband tariffs.

### S55 — TRAI: Press Releases
- **URL:** https://www.trai.gov.in/notifications/press-release
- **Frequency:** Weekly / ad-hoc
- **Key Data:** Regulatory announcements, new recommendations, subscriber highlights, policy updates
- **Reliability:** High
- **Access:** Free / no registration

### S56 — Department of Telecommunications (DoT)
- **URL:** https://dot.gov.in/
- **Frequency:** Ad-hoc
- **Key Data:** Spectrum auction rules, license conditions, FDI policy, USO fund data, BharatNet progress, telecom manufacturing policy
- **Reliability:** High
- **Access:** Free / no registration
- **Notes:** Key for tracking spectrum auction timelines and rules. Draft Telecommunications (Assignment of Spectrum through Auction) Rules, 2025 published. DoT administers the Telecommunications Act, 2023.

### S57 — Ministry of Communications: Budget & Policy
- **URL:** https://www.indiabudget.gov.in/doc/eb/sbe13.pdf (Demand No. 13)
- **Frequency:** Annual (Union Budget)
- **Key Data:** Budget allocation to DoT, BSNL capital infusion, USOF allocations, BharatNet spend, spectrum auction revenue estimates
- **Reliability:** High
- **Access:** Free / no registration
- **Notes:** FY2026-27 allocation: INR 73,990 crore for DoT, driven by BSNL support and BharatNet. PRS Legislative Research provides excellent demand-for-grants analysis.

### S58 — PRS Legislative Research: Telecom Budget Analysis
- **URL:** https://prsindia.org/budgets/parliament/demand-for-grants-2025-26-analysis-telecommunications
- **Frequency:** Annual
- **Key Data:** Analysis of telecom budget allocations, USOF trends, BSNL capex, BharatNet progress, policy priorities
- **Reliability:** High
- **Access:** Free / no registration
- **Notes:** Independent, non-partisan analysis of telecom budgets. Also covers FY2026-27 analysis at https://prsindia.org/files/budget/budget_parliament/2026/DfG_Analysis_2026-27-Telecom.pdf

## 2. Industry Bodies & Associations

### S59 — COAI (Cellular Operators Association of India)
- **URL:** https://www.coai.com/home
- **Frequency:** Annual report + ad-hoc policy papers
- **Key Data:** Annual industry reports, tariff analysis, ARPU trends, spectrum policy positions, financial sustainability assessments, AGR analysis
- **Reliability:** Medium-High (industry advocacy body; data may have operator bias)
- **Access:** Free (annual reports) / some content requires membership
- **Notes:** Core members: Bharti Airtel, Reliance Jio, Vodafone Idea. Annual report available at https://coai.com/coai-annual-report. Represents the voice of private telecom operators in regulatory consultations.

### S60 — AUSPI (Association of Unified Telecom Service Providers of India)
- **URL:** Not publicly maintained (submissions available on TRAI website)
- **Frequency:** Ad-hoc (regulatory submissions)
- **Key Data:** Policy positions on licensing, spectrum, AGR, interconnection; member operator perspectives
- **Reliability:** Medium (advocacy body)
- **Access:** Free (via TRAI submissions)
- **Notes:** Historically represented CDMA operators and ISPs. Less prominent than COAI in current landscape but still participates in regulatory consultations.

### S61 — GSMA Intelligence
- **URL:** https://www.gsmaintelligence.com/
- **Frequency:** Continuous (updated database) + annual Mobile Economy report
- **Key Data:** Unique subscriber counts, SIM penetration, 5G adoption curves, mobile internet penetration, revenue forecasts, operator financials, spectrum assignments
- **Reliability:** High
- **Access:** Paid subscription (premium data); some free research/blogs available
- **Notes:** Global gold-standard for mobile data. India-specific blog: "IMC 2025: India's Telecom Sector in Five Words." Annual "Mobile Economy" report (Feb 2025 edition) covers India projections. Mobile Connectivity Index tracks India's digital inclusion progress.

### S62 — DIPA (Digital Infrastructure Providers Association)
- **URL:** https://dipa.co.in/
- **Frequency:** Ad-hoc reports and policy submissions
- **Key Data:** Tower count (770,000+ towers, 2.7 million BTS), fiberization data, tower sharing ratios, RoW (Right of Way) policy, green energy adoption at tower sites
- **Reliability:** Medium-High
- **Access:** Free
- **Notes:** Formerly TAIPA (Tower and Infrastructure Providers Association), renamed to DIPA. Chaired by Prachur Sah (CEO, Indus Towers). Key voice for tower infrastructure policy.

- **Reliability:** Medium (limited public disclosure as unlisted entity)
- **Access:** Free (limited)
- **Notes:** State-owned. Not listed on stock exchanges, so financial disclosure is limited. Budget documents and parliamentary Q&As are better sources for BSNL financial data. INR 73,990 cr budget allocation for FY2026-27 includes significant BSNL support.

## 5. Data Providers

Interactive Mobility Visualizer at https://www.ericsson.com/en/reports-and-papers/mobility-report/mobility-visualizer allows custom data queries. November 2025 edition at https://www.ericsson.com/en/reports-and-papers/mobility-report/reports/november-2025.

### S77 — Statista: India Telecom
- **URL:** https://www.statista.com/topics/10820/telecommunications-industry-in-india/
