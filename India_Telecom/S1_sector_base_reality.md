## 1. Market Size & Growth

### 1.1 Revenue Overview

| Metric | FY24 | FY25 | YoY Growth |

| Gross Sector Revenue | Rs 3,36,066 Cr (~US$39.2B) | Rs 3,72,097 Cr (~US$43.4B) | +10.7% |
| Projected FY26 Revenue Growth | -- | -- | +7% est. |
| Projected FY27 Revenue Growth (post tariff hike) | -- | -- | +16% est. |

India's telecom sector generated gross revenue of approximately Rs 3,72,097 crore (US$43.4 billion) in FY25, representing 10.7% sequential growth over FY24 [S1]. India closed CY2024 with US$41 billion in telecom revenues, representing approximately 2.3% of the global telecom industry [S2]. Analysts forecast sector revenue growth to accelerate to 16% YoY in FY27, driven by an expected 15% tariff hike in mid-2026, compared to a more modest 7% growth rate in FY26 [S3].

India is the world's second-largest telecom market by subscriber count and the second-largest internet market globally [S4]. The total telephone subscriber base reached approximately 1.30 billion by December 2025, with wireless services accounting for the vast majority at 1.24 billion subscribers [S5].

### 1.3 ARPU Trends

| Operator | Q2 FY25 ARPU | Q2 FY26 ARPU | Q3 FY26 ARPU (est.) | Medium-Term Target |
|----------|-------------|-------------|---------------------|-------------------|
| Bharti Airtel | ~Rs 233 | Rs 256 | ~Rs 260+ | Rs 300 (post next hike); Rs 400 (5-year) |
| Reliance Jio | ~Rs 195 | Rs 211.4 | ~Rs 215+ | Rs 250+ (post next hike) |
| Vodafone Idea | ~Rs 166 | Rs 180 | ~Rs 185 | Rs 200+ (aspirational) |

ARPU has been on a structural uptrend since the post-Jio-entry trough of ~Rs 100 in 2018. Airtel leads the industry in ARPU at Rs 256 (Q2 FY26) and has a stated medium-term aspiration of Rs 300, with Morgan Stanley projecting Rs 400 over the next five years even without further tariff hikes beyond 2026 [S6]. Jio's ARPU reached Rs 211.4 in Q2 FY26. Both operators have been quietly phasing out entry-level 1GB-per-day plans, effectively nudging users toward higher-value 1.5GB-per-day packs starting at ~Rs 299 [S3].

## 3. Regulatory Environment

### 3.1 Key Regulatory Bodies and Framework

- **TRAI (Telecom Regulatory Authority of India):** Regulates tariffs, interconnection, quality of service, and spectrum recommendations. TRAI provides recommendations on spectrum auction reserve prices and bands to the DoT.
- **DoT (Department of Telecommunications):** Executes policy, licenses, and spectrum auctions. Reports to the Ministry of Communications.
- **Supreme Court of India:** Has played a critical role in AGR interpretation and spectrum-related disputes.

### 3.2 AGR Dues -- History and Current Status

The Adjusted Gross Revenue (AGR) dispute has been the single most consequential regulatory event in Indian telecom:

- **Origin:** The Supreme Court ruled in October 2019 that AGR calculations must include non-telecom revenue, creating massive retrospective liabilities for operators.
- **Impact on Vi:** Vodafone Idea's AGR dues were approximately Rs 76,000 crore (including accrued interest), threatening its viability.
- **2021 Relief Package:** Government offered a 4-year moratorium on AGR/spectrum dues with the option to convert interest into equity.
- **2025 Relief (Vi-specific):** The Centre froze Rs 87,695 crore in AGR dues for Vi until FY32 and appointed a DoT committee to reassess obligations. Vi will pay a maximum of Rs 124 crore annually between March 2026 and March 2031, with remaining dues settled in installments through March 2041 [S9].
- **Government Stake in Vi:** Through debt-to-equity conversion, the government now holds 49% of Vodafone Idea [S10].

### 3.3 Spectrum Auctions

- **2022 5G Auction:** Raised approximately Rs 1.5 lakh crore. Jio was the largest buyer (Rs 88,078 crore), acquiring spectrum across 700 MHz, 800 MHz, 1800 MHz, 3300 MHz, and 26 GHz bands.
- **2025 Spectrum Rules:** The Ministry of Communications issued the Telecommunications (Assignment of Spectrum through Auction) Rules, 2025, streamlining the auction process [S11].
- **FY27 Budget:** The government has pegged telecom sector revenues at Rs 1.17 trillion, including projected spectrum auction proceeds in 2026 [S12].
- **No Usage Charges:** Post-2022 spectrum acquisitions carry no spectrum usage charges, a major policy reform.

### 3.4 FDI Policy

- 100% FDI is permitted in the telecom sector (up to 49% through the automatic route; above 49% requires government approval).
- This liberal FDI policy facilitated Jio's fundraise of ~US$20 billion from global investors (Google, Meta, KKR, etc.) in 2020.

### 3.5 Universal Service Obligation Fund (USOF)

- USOF levies 5% of operators' Adjusted Gross Revenue to fund rural connectivity.
- The BharatNet project, funded partly through USOF, has connected over 250,000 Gram Panchayats via optical fiber, with ~220,000 service-ready as of early 2025 [S13].

## 7. Macro Drivers

### 7.1 Digital India Tailwinds

- **UPI Transactions:** 16-17 billion monthly UPI transactions, all reliant on mobile data connectivity [S23].
- **Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI):** Aadhaar, UPI, DigiLocker, and India Stack create a flywheel of digital adoption that increases telecom dependency.
- **Government e-Services:** Increasing digitization of government services (tax filing, subsidies, licenses) drives rural data demand.

### 7.2 Rural Penetration Opportunity

| Metric | Urban | Rural |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Internet Penetration | ~70%+ | ~35-37% |
| Mobile Penetration | 125.3% | 58.8% |
| 4G Coverage (Village Level) | ~100% | 88.9% |
| 5G Coverage (Village Level) | Near-universal | 77.8% |

Rural internet penetration at ~37% (up from 10% in 2015) represents the largest untapped growth opportunity [S24]. Rural areas are predicted to account for the majority of new internet user additions. The BharatNet project is extending fiber to 250,000+ Gram Panchayats.

### 7.3 Smartphone Adoption

- India's smartphone user base reached ~660 million in 2024 and is projected to cross 1 billion by 2025-26 [S25].
- Feature phone to smartphone migration remains a key driver of data adoption and ARPU uplift.
- Average smartphone prices are declining, with sub-Rs 7,000 (US$80) 4G smartphones widely available.
- 5G-capable smartphones are becoming mainstream, with prices falling below Rs 10,000 (US$120).

### 7.4 Jio IPO Catalyst

Reliance Jio is expected to conduct its IPO in H1 2026, potentially raising ~US$4-4.5 billion through a ~2.5% stake sale [S26]. Valuation estimates range from US$130 billion to US$240 billion, depending on the source. This would be India's largest-ever IPO and will establish a public market benchmark for the telecom sector. The IPO could trigger positive re-rating across listed telecom peers (Airtel, Vi, Indus Towers) as investors focus on sector fundamentals and comparable valuations [S27].

## 8. Structural Themes

### 8.1 Consolidation Arc

India has gone from 12+ operators in 2015 to effectively 3 private operators (and BSNL) by 2025. The market may further consolidate to a Jio-Airtel duopoly if Vi continues to deteriorate. This consolidation has transformed pricing power -- the industry has moved from destructive pricing wars (2016-2020) to coordinated tariff hikes (2021-present).

### 8.2 Tariff Hike Cycle

The industry has implemented multiple rounds of tariff increases since 2021:
- **December 2021:** 20-25% increase
- **July 2024:** 10-25% increase
- **Expected H1 2026:** 15-20% increase [S28]

Morgan Stanley forecasts a 16-20% tariff increase in 2026, which would drive 14% YoY ARPU growth in FY27 [S3].

### 8.3 From Connectivity to Platform

Both Jio and Airtel are evolving beyond pure-play telecom into digital platforms:
- **Jio:** JioMart (e-commerce), JioCinema (streaming), JioFinancial Services, JioCloud, Jio AI.
- **Airtel:** Airtel Payments Bank, Airtel Black (bundled services), Nxtra (data centers), enterprise cloud.

### 8.4 Global Context

India's telecom ARPU remains among the lowest globally (~US$2.50-3.00/month), even after multiple tariff hikes. For context, US ARPU is ~US$50/month, and even other developing markets like Indonesia and Brazil are at US$3-5/month. This suggests significant headroom for ARPU expansion over the medium-to-long term, especially as per-capita income rises.

## 9. Risk Factors

1. **Regulatory Overreach:** Government intervention on tariffs (e.g., floor pricing mandates) or AGR policy changes.
2. **Satellite Competition:** Starlink and Amazon Kuiper seeking SATCOM licenses in India; potential to disrupt rural broadband economics (though unlikely to impact urban mobile).
3. **Technology Risk:** Rapid shifts in 5G monetization models; risk that 5G capex does not generate proportional returns.
4. **Vi Collapse Scenario:** An uncontrolled Vi exit could disrupt pricing stability (though it would ultimately strengthen the duopoly).
5. **Macro/Currency Risk:** Rupee depreciation increases equipment costs (most network gear is imported in USD/EUR).
6. **Spectrum Liability Overhang:** Operators carry significant spectrum payment obligations over 20-year installment plans.
